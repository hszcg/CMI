tricky talks

2013 Oct

Speaker: Angela Zottarel (Aarhus)

Place: Tsinghua, Time: 2pm

View details »
  • Abstract

    • One-way functions (OWFs) and pseudorandom generators (PRGs) are two of the most important primtives of modern cryptography. Blum, Micali [BM82] and Yao [Yao82] independently gave an elegant construction of PRGs from one-way permutations (OWPs) with optimal parameters (linear seed length and a single call to the underlying OWP) which was known as the BMY generator. Later constructions were known for the more general cases such as from known regular OWFs [GKL93], from unknown regular OWFs [HHR06] and from any OWFs [HILL99,VZ12], but most of them are missing from the cryptography textbook partially due to the quite involved proofs. In this talk, I will briefly introduce previous works and then present a new approach to the PRG constructions from (both known and unknown) regular one-way function via the computational unpredictability (a useful notion in leakage-resilient cryptography) that either simplify the proofs given in the cryptography textbook or improve the current start-of-the-art constructions. These constructions are optimal in seed length and the number of OWF calls (by matching the recent lower bounds of [Holenstein and Sinha,FOCS12]). If time permits, i will also discuss why the HHR's randomized iterate doesn't work for an arbitrary OWF and extension of the technique to building universal one-way hash functions (UOWHFs) from regular one-way functions. No prerequisites are required.

  • Bio

    • In 2010, I graduated in mathematics as an Algant scholar, receiving a joint master degree from Padova and Leiden University. I joined the cryptography group at Aarhus university in August 2010, as a PhD student. My main supervisor is Jesper Buus Nielsel. My research field is mainly focused on hardness assumptions and leakage resilient public key cryptography. In summer 2012, I spent three months in Tokyo, doing an internship in Okamoto's group at NTT laboratories. From November 2012 to April 2013 I visited Leonid Reyzin at Boston University. I will hand-in my dissertation at the end of October 2013.
